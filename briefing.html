Claude Opus 4.7 lands with a 10-point jump on SWE-bench Pro — and lands on Bedrock, Foundry and Snowflake the same day

Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.7 with 64.3% on SWE-bench Pro (up from 53.4%), 87.6% on SWE-bench Verified, and 69.4% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 — all ahead of GPT-5.4. Pricing is unchanged at $5 / $25 per million tokens. The model ships with 3x higher image resolution, stronger long-horizon performance and new developer controls. It was simultaneously made available on AWS Bedrock, Microsoft Foundry and Snowflake Cortex AI.

Why it matters: Opus 4.7 cements Anthropic's lead on hard agentic coding and keeps pricing flat — pressure on OpenAI's next refresh. Enterprise distribution across all three hyperscaler clouds on day one is the real moat.
