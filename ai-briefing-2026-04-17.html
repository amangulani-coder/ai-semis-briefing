Google’s Gemma 4 Runs Fully Offline on iPhone — 31B Params, Apache 2.0

Google’s open-weights Gemma 4, released April 2 under Apache 2.0 with variants from 2.3B to 31B, is now running fully on-device on iPhone. The 31B dense model already ranks #3 on Arena AI among open models — and is doing it with zero cloud calls.

Why it matters: Bundling a frontier model with a content-creation surface moves Anthropic off the pure-API playing field and into direct, rendered end-user territory — where OpenAI has been dominant.

TSMC Q1 Net Profit Jumps 58% to Record as AI Demand Stays “Extremely Robust”

TSMC reported a record Q1 Thursday: net profit NT$572.48B ($18.11B), up 58% YoY, on $35.9B of revenue. High-performance computing climbed to 61% of revenue (from 55% last quarter), 3nm was 25% of wafers, gross margin 66%. CEO C.C. Wei: “AI-related demand continues to be extremely robust.” Full-year revenue guide lifted to +30% YoY.

Why it matters: TSMC's print is the single cleanest read-through on 2026 AI capex. With packaging capacity so tight TSMC has outsourced simpler steps, supply — not demand — is setting the ceiling.
Sources: Yahoo Finance · Digitimes · TipRanks

ASML Lifts 2026 Outlook to €36–40B as AI Orders Stay “Very Strong”

ASML raised its 2026 net-sales guide to €36–40B (from €34–39B) on April 15, with Q1 sales of €8.76B and net profit €2.76B beating consensus. CEO Fouquet cited “ongoing AI-related infrastructure investments” and “very strong” order intake. ASML plans to ship 60 low-NA EUV tools in 2026, up 25% YoY, with capacity for 80 in 2027.

Why it matters: A trillion-dollar two-year pipeline effectively pre-books the bulk of hyperscaler AI capex, leaving AMD and custom silicon fighting for incremental share rather than the core slot.
Source: CNBC

NVIDIA Q4 Revenue $68.1B — Data Center +75%, Q1 Guide $78B

NVIDIA closed FY2026 with $68.13B of Q4 revenue, +73% YoY, driven by $62.31B in Data Center (+75%). Q1 FY2027 guidance points to roughly $78B. Taken with this week’s TSMC and ASML prints, the AI build-out still has no visible ceiling — both foundries are now openly saying demand is outpacing supply.

Why it matters: If AMD can close the inference gap on memory-bound workloads, hyperscalers finally have a credible second supplier — and Lisa Su a real answer to the NVIDIA lock-in.
Source: FinancialContent
