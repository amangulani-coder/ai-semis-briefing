🏭 Strategy
TSMC Arizona Emerges as Tariff-Exempt Supply Channel — US Fabs Gain Strategic Value

As tariffs reshape global supply chains, TSMC's Arizona GigaFab is being repositioned as a tariff-exempt source of advanced chips for US customers. Products manufactured in Arizona would be classified as US-made, avoiding import tariffs and qualifying for CHIPS Act subsidies. NVIDIA, Apple, and AMD — all of which have locked capacity at Arizona — may see competitive advantages from their US-footprint investments. The 10% minimum global tariff took effect Saturday morning.

🏭 Intel
Intel 18A and TSMC Arizona: Why US-Made Chips Gain in a Tariff World

Intel's 18A high-volume manufacturing achievement and TSMC's Arizona GigaFab both become strategically more valuable in a tariff environment. Chips manufactured in the US avoid import duties and qualify for CHIPS Act benefits. Microsoft, Amazon, and NVIDIA have all locked in Arizona capacity — a decision that looks prescient after Friday's tariff shock. Intel's 18A process is also now positioned as a viable domestic alternative to TSMC Taiwan.

⚠️ GPU Prices
GPU Prices Up 5–15% from Tariffs — AI Buildout Costs Surge for Hyperscalers

GPU prices rose 5–15% across segments as tariff costs hit the supply chain. For hyperscalers building AI data centers, a 10% increase in GPU costs translates to hundreds of millions in additional capex per cluster. Companies that pre-ordered or locked in supply agreements before April 2 are insulated; those still procuring on spot markets face the full tariff premium. This accelerates the move to custom ASICs made in tariff-exempt jurisdictions.
