Lead Stories 03
MARKETS · NVIDIA
Jensen Huang$5T Market CapVera Rubin

NVIDIA closes Friday at a record $208.27 (+4.3%), pushing market cap past $5 trillion for the first time

NVDA capped a four-week melt-up on April 24 with the iShares Semiconductor ETF (SOXX) up 40.4% month-to-date. The cross of the $5T line — a level no public company had ever closed above — was driven by 75%+ Data Center growth, the OpenAI/Oracle/Microsoft infra deals signed earlier this quarter, and renewed confidence that the Vera Rubin platform ramps on schedule. Friday's tape sets up a high-stakes earnings week for Microsoft, Alphabet, Meta, Amazon and Apple.

INTEL · Q1 EARNINGS
Lip-Bu Tan+24% in a day

Intel posts best single-day stock move since 1987 — Q1 revenue $13.58B beats; Data Center & AI +22% YoY to $5.05B

Intel's Friday print silenced the bears: $13.58B revenue (+7.18% YoY) topped Street, and the Data Center & AI segment hit $5.05B with strong Xeon 6 and Gaudi traction. The 24% one-day pop is the largest INTC daily gain since the Black Monday era, and it dragged Philadelphia Semiconductor Index to a new all-time high.

ANTHROPIC · MEGADEAL
$65BAmazon + Google

Anthropic stacks $65B in fresh commitments — Amazon adds $25B, Google pledges up to $40B on Friday

A staggered week: Amazon committed up to $25B as part of an expanded AWS partnership, then Google followed Friday with a multi-year compute and equity package worth up to $40B. The two pledges effectively bankroll Anthropic's Mythos and Claude roadmaps through the next two model cycles, even as both hyperscalers continue to back rivals.

Semiconductors 03
TSMC · A13 / N2U
TSMCAI nodes

TSMC unveils A13 and N2U process nodes — next-gen geometry pitched squarely at 2027 AI accelerators

On April 22, TSMC introduced its A13 family alongside an N2U variant aimed at AI compute. The roadmap update lands as analysts now expect AI chips to drive about a third of TSMC sales by 2027, with revenue projected to climb from ~$164B in 2026 to ~$204B in 2027 and ~$311B by 2030. Notably absent: any commitment yet on ASML high-NA EUV adoption, which TSMC continues to defer on cost grounds.

AMD · CHIP RALLY
Lisa Su+14% in a day

AMD jumps 14% to $349.54 — MI400 momentum and the Intel halo lift the stock to a fresh high

Advanced Micro Devices closed Friday up 14% as AI-data-center read-throughs from Intel's print rippled across the sector. AMD is now +42.6% YTD, with Street models still treating MI400 as upside. The setup leaves Lisa Su with a clean runway into the May earnings update.

EDA · CADENCE
CadenceTSMC + NVIDIA + Google

Cadence deepens AI-EDA pact with TSMC, NVIDIA and Google — agentic flows certified for N3, N2, A16 and A14

Cadence Design Systems expanded its joint design framework this week, bringing agentic-AI-assisted IP and certified flows to TSMC's leading-edge nodes. The partnership is meaningful for hyperscaler ASIC pipelines: Google's next TPU generations and a wave of NVIDIA custom designs all sit on these process technologies.

AI & Policy 03
OPENAI · NVIDIA
Sam AltmanCodex10,000 employees

NVIDIA rolls out OpenAI Codex to all 10,000 employees — Huang frames AI agents as "teammates," not just dev tools

NVIDIA confirmed this week it has deployed OpenAI's Codex coding agent across its entire global workforce — engineering, design, ops and supporting roles. Jensen Huang called it a milestone in "the age of AI"; Sam Altman replied that early company-wide testing "was awesome." The move is the largest single-company Codex deployment to date and a public flex on agentic productivity ahead of Q2 earnings season.

ANTHROPIC · MYTHOS
SecurityMythos 5

Cybersecurity researchers raise alarm over unauthorized access to Anthropic's Mythos model — exfiltration risk being assessed

Reports late this week describe parties with unauthorized access to Mythos, Anthropic's 10-trillion-parameter cybersecurity-and-coding model. Defenders worry attackers may operationalize the model before the broader patch and detection ecosystem catches up. Anthropic has not detailed scope; this is the first known incident of that scale on a frontier model.

EU · DIGITAL RULES
European CommissionOpenAI

EU set to classify ChatGPT as a Very Large Online Platform — strictest tier of obligations would apply to OpenAI

Per Handelsblatt sources inside the European Commission, ChatGPT is days away from being designated under the DSA's VLOP regime. The classification carries audit, transparency and risk-mitigation duties, and would put OpenAI under the same supervisory umbrella as Google Search and Meta's feeds — a meaningful escalation in EU oversight of generative AI.
