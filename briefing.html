Anthropic Launches Project Glasswing — $100M Cybersecurity Coalition

Anthropic unveiled Project Glasswing, a 12-partner coalition including AWS, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorganChase, the Linux Foundation, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and Palo Alto Networks. The initiative commits up to $100M in model usage credits plus $4M in open-source security donations. Partners gain exclusive access to Claude Mythos Preview — an unreleased frontier model that has already identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser before attackers could exploit them. Mythos Preview remains restricted to approved critical-infrastructure maintainers and will not be made generally available.

Why It Matters First time a leading AI lab has mobilised a frontier model exclusively for proactive, pre-disclosure vulnerability research. Sets a precedent for AI-first security operations at scale and signals Anthropic's expanding enterprise moat beyond general assistants.

Jensen Huang & Lisa Su Join PCAST — Chip Executives Advise White House

TSMC Q1 2026: $35.8B Revenue Guidance, +38% YoY

TSMC guided Q1 2026 revenue at $34.6B–$35.8B, representing 38% year-over-year growth driven by the maturity of 3nm and 5nm nodes plus the early ramp of 2nm Gate-All-Around (GAA) technology. Gross margins are expected between 63–65%; operating margins 54–56%. Full-year capex guidance stands at a record $52B–$56B, with 70–80% allocated to leading-edge nodes. Earnings call: April 16.

China GPU Market Export Controls

China's Domestic GPU Share Climbs Amid Deepening Export Controls

New data shows China's domestic GPU vendors — led by Cambricon, Biren, and Huawei's Ascend division — now account for a meaningful share of AI accelerator deployments within China as export restrictions on NVIDIA H20 and equivalent chips continue to tighten. Chinese cloud providers are accelerating qualification of domestic alternatives, though performance parity with leading NVIDIA parts remains a 1–2 generation gap at inference workloads.

Why It Matters China's forced self-sufficiency in AI compute is creating a genuine parallel hardware ecosystem. In the long run this bifurcates the global AI stack — different chips, different software, different optimisation paths — with major implications for global model compatibility and frontier research access.

Aria Networks Series B AI Networking

Aria Networks Raises $125M Series B for AI-Native Networking Stack

Aria Networks closed a $125M Series B to accelerate development of its AI-native networking infrastructure stack, which aims to replace legacy switching and routing for hyperscale AI training clusters. The round was led by Sequoia Capital with participation from NVIDIA Ventures and existing investors. Aria claims its software-defined fabric reduces fat-tree network congestion in all-to-all collective workloads by up to 40% versus incumbent approaches.

Why It Matters Network performance is increasingly the binding constraint in scaling large AI training runs. Purpose-built AI networking startups like Aria (and rivals Enfabrica, Ayar Labs) are attracting major capital as hyperscalers look beyond Infiniband and standard Ethernet.
