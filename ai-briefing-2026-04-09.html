Meta Debuts Muse Spark — First Model From New Superintelligence Labs

Meta unveiled Muse Spark, its first major AI model since the $14B acquisition of Scale AI’s Alexandr Wang nine months ago. Originally code-named Avocado, the model is the inaugural release from the new Muse series developed by Meta Superintelligence Labs. The creative AI platform targets content creators, designers, and marketing teams with integrated generative image, video, and audio tools within Meta’s advertising ecosystem. META shares rose +3.8% on the announcement.

Nvidia Locks Up TSMC Advanced Packaging as U.S. Capacity Ramps

Nvidia has reserved the majority of TSMC’s leading CoWoS advanced packaging technology as AI chip packaging emerges as the next critical bottleneck. TSMC is ramping two new packaging facilities in Taiwan plus two in Arizona. Capacity is so heavily booked that TSMC has outsourced steps to ASE and Amkor. Meanwhile, Elon Musk has tapped Intel to package custom chips for SpaceX, xAI, and Tesla at his planned Texas Terafab.

OpenAI, Anthropic & Google Unite Against Chinese AI Model Theft

The three leading Western AI labs announced they are sharing intelligence through the Frontier Model Forum to combat Chinese companies stealing their models via adversarial distillation. Anthropic alone documented 16 million unauthorized exchanges from three named Chinese firms — DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax — conducted through roughly 24,000 fraudulent accounts. The coordinated response marks the first time these rivals have formally collaborated on IP defense.

Why It Matters Model distillation theft threatens the economics of frontier AI development. If competitors can replicate capabilities at a fraction of R&D cost, the incentive structure for billion-dollar training runs erodes. This alliance could also influence upcoming U.S. policy on AI IP protection and export controls.

TSMC Faces Severe 3nm Shortage — Fabs Sold Out Through 2028

TSMC’s 3nm fabs are completely sold out through 2028, forcing the company to plan a massive “GigaFab” expansion in Arizona and launch 3nm production in Japan to meet relentless global demand. The shortage reflects surging orders from Apple, Nvidia, Qualcomm, and AMD as AI workloads drive unprecedented demand for leading-edge silicon.

Why It Matters A multi-year sold-out node is historically unusual and underscores the structural supply deficit in advanced semiconductors. The GigaFab and Japan expansions represent TSMC’s most aggressive geographic diversification ever.

Samsung Plans Record $73B Investment in 2026 — Outpacing TSMC Capex

Samsung is planning a record $73 billion capital investment in 2026, surpassing TSMC’s approximately $52B capex guidance. The aggressive spend targets advanced foundry, HBM memory, and AI chip packaging as Samsung fights to close the manufacturing gap with TSMC.

Why It Matters The Samsung vs. TSMC capex war signals that the foundry duopoly will intensify rather than consolidate. Samsung’s willingness to outspend TSMC hints at aggressive pricing or yield commitments to lure customers away from the market leader.
