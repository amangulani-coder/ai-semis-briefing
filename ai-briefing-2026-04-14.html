Anthropic Unveils Claude Mythos 5 with 10 Trillion Parameters

Anthropic launched Claude Mythos 5, a 10T-parameter frontier model tuned for advanced cybersecurity and autonomous coding workloads, alongside a lighter consumer model called Capabara. Early red-team reports say Mythos can surface vulnerabilities in legacy banking and core infrastructure stacks within minutes.

Why it matters: Mythos pushes the frontier past Gemini 3.1 and GPT-5.4 on several autonomous-coding benchmarks, but regulators are already flagging the banking-infra discovery capability as dual-use — offensive security teams and attackers both benefit.

Meta Debuts Muse Spark, First Major Model from Superintelligence Labs

Meta released Muse Spark (code-named Avocado), the first major model out of Meta Superintelligence Labs under Alexandr Wang. It is Meta's first real counter to OpenAI and Google since the $14.3B Scale AI deal that brought Wang to Menlo Park.

Why it matters: After Llama 4's tepid reception, Meta needed a flagship. Muse Spark is Wang's debut and a signal that the $14B bet on his talent is starting to ship product.

TSMC Set for Fourth Straight Record Quarter as AI Demand Roars

TSMC is expected to report Q1 net profit of T$542.6B (~$17.1B), a 50% YoY surge, on insatiable AI infrastructure demand. Q1 revenue rose 35% YoY and advanced CoWoS packaging grew ~80% YoY — most of that capacity already reserved by NVIDIA.

Why it matters: TSMC results drop ahead of NVIDIA earnings and are the cleanest read on AI capex trajectory. Another beat here reinforces the 2026–2027 $1T data-center chip story NVIDIA sketched at GTC.

NVIDIA Locks Advanced Packaging as TSMC Expands US Footprint

NVIDIA has scooped up additional CoWoS packaging capacity from TSMC while TSMC prepares to break ground on two new Arizona fabs. The capacity deal is tied to the Rubin R100 ramp on TSMC N3P with HBM4 at 22 TB/s.

Why it matters: Packaging, not wafers, is the real bottleneck for AI chips. NVIDIA reserving CoWoS lines squeezes every other fabless customer — including AMD and the hyperscaler custom-silicon programs.

AMD Guides to Multi-Year Revenue Surge Through 2030

Top Labs Form Security Pact to Share Internal Model Defenses

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have agreed to share their most sensitive internal model-security data through the Frontier Model Forum, responding to a rise in model-cloning and distillation attempts originating overseas. The labs will pool red-team findings, weight-exfiltration indicators, and pre-release eval results.

Why it matters: Fierce commercial rivals collaborating on security is a strong signal that frontier-lab leadership sees nation-state threats as existential. Expect regulatory tailwind — this mirrors what DC has been asking for.
Source: humai.blog · Frontier Model Forum

Huang, Su, Ellison Join White House Tech Council — Altman and Musk Excluded

The Trump administration finalized a new AI & tech advisory council that includes Jensen Huang (NVIDIA), Lisa Su (AMD), Larry Ellison (Oracle), and Mark Zuckerberg (Meta). Conspicuously absent: Sam Altman (OpenAI), Microsoft executives, and Elon Musk.

Why it matters: The guest list is the story. Chip-layer CEOs are in; the highest-profile model-layer CEOs are out. Expect policy to lean toward domestic manufacturing incentives rather than model-level oversight.

Banks Brace as Mythos Probes Legacy Core Systems

Security researchers report that Anthropic's new Mythos model, given a narrowly scoped test harness, flagged undisclosed vulnerabilities in legacy banking middleware within hours. Several Tier-1 banks have reportedly paused sandbox evaluations until patches ship.

Why it matters: Banking IT runs on decades-old COBOL and vendor stacks. An AI that can map those attack surfaces at machine speed is a step-change — this is the first real "autonomous red team" moment the industry has flinched at.
